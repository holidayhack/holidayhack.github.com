The Holiday Hack Project

Are you looking to do some paid programming work on your free time? Well, here's the gist of it:

I will pay you ฿2,500 every week for 2 days worth of concentrated in-the-zone coding. You chose where, when, how and how long to work and I will pay you cash for a patch and some chit-chat every other Saturdays.

That's ฿10,000 a month. Continue reading if you're interested.

One coder army

Since one coder does not scale and I have a full time job now just like you, I figured it is better that I hire other people to help than to try to do everything myself and never gets anything done.

Think of this as me doing micro-funding for my ideas.

I am a coder myself, so you can expect a lot of freedom to go about how you do the job. Plus I understand how it is so much better to work alone on your own terms than on a team of sub-averages who don't understand the simplest of your approach.

Conditions

  • If we have never met before, you will have to do some coding tests first (such as a FUBAR loop) so I know we can communicate.

  • ...and that you're not a Blub programmer.

  • Bad coding style will be rejected on first sight. If you don't understand what that means, please close this website immediately.
  • The constant here is the weekly pay of ฿2,500. Feel free to spend only 2 hour(s) per week if you think you can match that amount. Just remember that I'm a coder too.
  • Skip any week whenever you want (pay for that week will be skipped.) Just don't disappear for too long without at least an email or a tweet.
  • There is nothing to sign. No NDAs. No legal documents bullshit. We prefer Meritocracy over legalities.
  • Also mean that anyone may stop working from either side at any time, should the work (or pay) be unsatisfactory.
  • Oh, and you have to be able to meet me in person in Bankgok, Thailand. (hint: look at the domain :-)

Can I use Ruby/Python/Node.JS/X?

Sure, use whatever you are good at. Personally, though, I hate PHP and Java so try not to use it. An all-OSS stack is also preferred (but not required.)

Cloud-hostable solution such as Heroku, OpenShift or Nodejitsu is preferred.

We will share the responsibility of any deployment and keeping the software up and running if the project have a server component.

How do I get paid?

In cash, in face-to-face meeting, every Saturdays in exchange for the week's patch and half-an-hour of talk.

What about sharing?

Sure, if you think the project is a good idea to invest, I'm willing to share a large portion (up to 50%) of it with you. We can negotiate this as soon as we have something viable for launch.

What to prepare?

I may give you a coding test to find out about your skills. If you got it, please take the time to do it in a concise, elegant, clean and performant way. Remember that bad coding style is a big no-no with me.

If you have decided to email me and we have scheduled a meeting, please be prepared by bringing some reference to your old work and also to talk about it at length as well. Unpreparedness is a sign of a lack of interest.
