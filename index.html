The Holiday Hack Project

Do you want to work on awesome programming projects in your free time and get paid while doing it?

I will pay you ฿2,500 for an approximated two-days worth of concentrated in-the-zone coding every saturdays, every weeks.

That's ฿10,000 a month! Continue reading if you are interested.

How does this works?

  1. You send me one pull request at the end of each week and we do a mini code review and demo session.
  2. A little chit-chat of what have we been up to and what should we be doing next. Your pull request gets merged and perhaps, deployed.
  3. I give you the week's pay and we're on our way.

No mid-week harassments or interruptions. No 3AM calls. Work where you want, whenever you want. Send good pull request, meetup and get paid, nothing else is needed.

One coder army

Since one coder does not scale and I have a full time job now just like you, I figured it is better that I hire other people to help than to try to do everything myself and never gets anything done.

Think of this as me doing a lot of micro startups.

I am a coder myself, so you can expect a lot of freedom to go about how you do the job. Plus I understand how it is so much better to work alone on your own terms than on a team of sub-averages who don't understand the simplest of your approach.

Conditions

  1. You must be able to speak and listen at least average English. We may also consider some level of Tinglish.
  2. You have to do some live coding test such as a FUBAR loop or a data structure implementation before you will be accepted. The exact test may varies according to my judgement.
  3. Things that will not be tolerated includes: blub programmer, bad coding style, arrogance and smart-asses. If you have any issue with any of these, please do not bother applying.
  4. The constant here is the weekly pay of ฿2,500 and a good pull request. Feel free to skew the time if you believe you have completed a worthwhile work.
  5. Unlimited holidays. Skip out whenever you want. You will simply not be paid for the week. Just don't disappear for too long without at least an email.
  6. We prefer Meritocracy over legalities. We keep all legal document bullshit to the barest minimum.
  7. Also mean that anyone may stop working from either side at any time, should the work (or pay) be unsatisfactory.
  8. Oh, and you have to be able to meet me in person in Bankgok, Thailand. (hint: look at the domain :-)

Can I use Ruby, Python, Node.JS or X?

Sure, use whatever you are good at. Personally, though, I hate PHP and Java so try not to use it.

An all-OSS stack is preferred as well as cloud-hostable solution such as Heroku, OpenShift, Nodejitsu or even Amazon EC2 with Puppet.

I will expect you to always be up to the latest trends including knowledge of their practicality and applicability and how it effects your preferred technology stack.

We will share the responsibility of any deployment and keeping the software up and running if the project have a server component.

How do I get paid?

In cash, in face-to-face meeting, every Saturdays in exchange for the week's pull request and half-an-hour of talk.

What about sharing?

Sure, if you think the project is a good idea to invest, I'm willing to share a large portion (up to 50%) of it with you in addition to the weekly constant. We can negotiate this as soon as we have something viable for launch.
